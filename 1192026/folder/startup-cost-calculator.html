How This Calculator Works

One-Time Costs are initial investments you pay once (equipment, legal setup, website).

Monthly Recurring Costs are ongoing expenses you'll pay every month.

Cash Runway is reserve money to keep the business running before you reach profitability. 6 months is typical for bootstrapped startups; investors often want 12-18 months.

Total Capital Needed is the complete amount you should have before launching.

Formulas Used

  • Total One-Time Costs = Sum of all one-time items
  • Total Monthly Costs = Sum of all monthly items
  • Cash Runway = Monthly Costs × Runway Months
  • Total Capital Needed = One-Time Costs + Cash Runway

Example Calculation

Frequently Asked Questions

What's included in one-time startup costs?

Equipment, furniture, initial inventory, legal/licensing fees, website development, business registration, initial marketing materials, and deposits.

How much cash runway should I have?

Bootstrapped startups typically need 6 months. If seeking investors, plan for 12-18 months. More runway = less risk.

What are monthly recurring costs?

Rent, payroll, software subscriptions, utilities, insurance, marketing, loan payments, and any other regular monthly expenses.

Should I add a buffer to my total?

Yes. Add 10-20% to your final total for unexpected costs. Things always cost more than planned.
