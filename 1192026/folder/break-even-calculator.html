How This Calculator Works

Break-Even Point is when your total revenue equals total costs — you're not making money yet, but you're not losing it either.

Contribution Margin is what's left from each sale after covering variable costs. This money goes toward fixed costs and profit.

Break-Even Units shows exactly how many sales you need before you start profiting.

Formulas Used

  • Contribution Margin = Price Per Unit - Variable Cost Per Unit
  • Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin
  • Break-Even Revenue = Break-Even Units × Price Per Unit
  • Contribution Margin Ratio = (Contribution Margin ÷ Price) × 100

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a break-even point?

The break-even point is the level of sales at which total revenue equals total costs. At this point, you're neither making a profit nor a loss.

What's the difference between fixed and variable costs?

Fixed costs stay the same regardless of sales (rent, insurance). Variable costs change with each unit sold (materials, shipping).

Is a lower break-even point better?

Yes. A lower break-even point means you need fewer sales to become profitable, which reduces risk and improves cash flow.

How often should I calculate my break-even point?

Recalculate whenever costs or prices change, when launching new products, or during business planning cycles (quarterly or annually).
