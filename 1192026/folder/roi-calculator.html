How This Calculator Works

ROI (Return on Investment) shows the percentage profit you made on your investment.

  • ROI = 100% means you doubled your money (spent $1000, gained $1000 profit = $2000 total)
  • ROI = 50% means you made $1.50 for every $1 spent
  • ROI = -20% means you lost money

Positive ROI = profitable campaign. Negative ROI = losing money.

Return Multiple is easier to visualize: 3.0x means every $1 spent returned $3 in revenue.

Formulas Used

  • Net Profit = Revenue - Investment - COGS
  • ROI Percentage = (Net Profit ÷ Investment) × 100
  • Return Multiple = Revenue ÷ Investment
  • Profit Per Dollar = Net Profit ÷ Investment

Frequently Asked Questions

What's a good ROI for marketing?

A 5:1 ratio (500% ROI) is considered strong for most marketing campaigns. 3:1 (300%) is acceptable. Below 2:1 (200%) may not be sustainable.

Should I include COGS in my ROI calculation?

Yes, for accuracy. If you're selling physical products, include COGS. For services with minimal costs, you can leave it at $0.

How do I improve my ROI?

Increase revenue (better targeting, higher conversion), reduce ad spend (optimize campaigns), or reduce COGS (better suppliers, volume discounts).

What if my ROI is negative?

Stop the campaign immediately. Analyze what went wrong (poor targeting, weak offer, wrong audience) before spending more.
