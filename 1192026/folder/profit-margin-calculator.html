How This Calculator Works

Gross Profit Margin shows what percentage of revenue remains after covering direct costs (COGS). A 40% margin means you keep $0.40 of every dollar before operating expenses.

Net Profit Margin shows what percentage you keep after ALL expenses. A 15% net margin is healthy for most small businesses.

Markup shows how much you charge above cost. A 50% markup means you sell at 1.5× your cost.

Formulas Used

  • Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS
  • Gross Profit Margin = (Gross Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100
  • Net Profit = Revenue - COGS - Operating Expenses
  • Net Profit Margin = (Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100
  • Markup = ((Revenue - COGS) ÷ COGS) × 100

Frequently Asked Questions

What's a good profit margin for a small business?

Net profit margins of 10-20% are typical for small businesses. Service businesses often have higher margins (20-40%) than retail (2-10%).

What's the difference between margin and markup?

Margin is profit as a percentage of sales price. Markup is profit as a percentage of cost. A 50% markup equals a 33% margin.

How can I improve my profit margins?

Increase prices, reduce COGS (negotiate with suppliers, buy in bulk), reduce operating expenses, or shift to higher-margin products/services.

Should I focus on gross or net profit margin?

Both matter. Gross margin shows pricing effectiveness. Net margin shows overall business health after all costs.
