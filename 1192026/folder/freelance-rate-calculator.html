How This Calculator Works

Minimum Hourly Rate covers your salary, taxes, and expenses — but leaves no profit margin. You'll just break even.

Recommended Rate includes a profit buffer (typically 15-25%) for business growth, slow months, and unexpected costs.

Why 30 billable hours? Most freelancers work 40+ hours but only 20-35 are billable (rest is admin, marketing, etc.).

Formulas Used

  • Working Weeks = 52 - Vacation Weeks
  • Annual Billable Hours = Billable Hours × Working Weeks
  • Salary After Tax = Desired Salary ÷ (1 - Tax Rate)
  • Total Needs = Salary After Tax + Expenses
  • Minimum Rate = Total Needs ÷ Annual Hours
  • Recommended Rate = Minimum Rate ÷ (1 - Profit Margin)

Frequently Asked Questions

What are billable hours?

Billable hours are the time you can actually charge clients. If you work 40 hours/week, typically only 20-35 are billable (the rest is admin, marketing, proposals).

Why do I need a profit margin?

A profit margin (15-25%) provides a buffer for slow months, business growth, equipment upgrades, and unexpected expenses. Without it, one slow month puts you in the red.

Is this rate too high for my market?

If your calculated rate seems high, you have three options: reduce expenses, increase billable hours, or accept a lower salary. Don't undercharge — it's not sustainable.

Should I charge the same rate for all clients?

This is your baseline rate. You can charge more for rush work, difficult clients, or premium services. Never charge less than your minimum rate.
